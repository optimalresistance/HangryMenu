Shish Barak — Homemade Meat Dumplings in Warm Yogurt Sauce ▾

Ingredients
Dough
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2–3 tbsp olive oil
  • ~½ cup warm water (add slowly until dough forms)
Meat Filling
  • 250 g ground beef
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 tbsp pine nuts (optional but authentic)
  • ½ tsp allspice
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • Salt & pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
Yogurt Sauce
  • 1½–2 cups plain yogurt
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 egg (stabilizes yogurt, prevents curdling)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • ½ tsp dried mint
  • Salt to taste
Side
  • 1 cup cooked vermicelli rice
Recipe
  1. Make the dough: Mix flour, salt, and olive oil. Add warm water gradually until a soft dough forms. Knead 5 minutes, cover, and rest 20 minutes.
  2. Cook the filling: Sauté onion in olive oil until soft. Add beef, pine nuts, allspice, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Cook until browned. Cool slightly.
  3. Shape dumplings: Roll dough thin. Cut small circles (2 inches). Place 1 tsp filling inside each, fold into half-moon, pinch edges, then fold corners to form the classic Shish Barak “hat”.
  4. Lightly bake or pan-sear: Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 5–8 minutes until pale golden—OR pan-sear 3 minutes. Do not over-brown.
  5. Make the yogurt sauce: Whisk yogurt, egg, cornstarch, and ½ cup water until smooth. Cook over medium heat, stirring continuously in one direction until it gently simmers.
  6. Season: Add garlic, dried mint, and salt. Simmer 5 minutes.
  7. Add dumplings: Drop the baked dumplings into the sauce and cook 5–7 minutes until fully tender and flavored.
  8. Serve: Spoon over warm vermicelli rice.
Dinner Serves 3
